Overview

Grow Up Green is a responsive resale platform designed to make secondhand shopping for children intuitive, trustworthy, and emotionally rewarding. I designed a browsable experience with swipe-style interactions, eco-conscious branding, and accessibility-first choices that highlight sustainability and community impact.

My Role

I owned the full end-to-end design process: research planning, competitive analysis, persona development, wireframing, prototyping, usability testing, and iteration. I also developed the brand‚Äôs color palette, typography, and responsive visual system.

The Challenge

As a first-time aunt, I saw my sisters struggle to afford baby clothes while managing constant growth spurts and tight budgets. At the same time, my own experience reselling on Depop showed me the gap: there was no resale platform designed specifically for kids that felt easy, trustworthy, and values-driven.

Parents were interested in secondhand shopping but frustrated by existing platforms. Through early conversations and observation, I identified three barriers:

  • Browsing and listing take too much time.
  • Concerns about hygiene and seller trust create hesitation.
  • There is little social or emotional incentive to choose used over new.

This led to my guiding question:

How might we design a secondhand shopping experience for kids that is fast, trustworthy, and emotionally rewarding?

My vision was to rebrand secondhand as purposeful and modern‚Äîcombining the simplicity of Facebook Marketplace with the positive reinforcement of apps like Ecosia and Forest.

User Research and Insights

I conducted five in-person interviews with parents, grandparents, and caregivers. Each was guided through a simulated shopping task‚Äîsearching, filtering, adding items to cart, and checking out‚Äîwhile comparing the experience to platforms they already used.

Key Insights

  • Trust is essential: Parents wanted verification of sellers, cleanliness, and authenticity.
  • Efficiency drives usage: 5/5 participants said they would leave if they couldn‚Äôt find items within minutes.
  • Details build confidence: Users requested clearer sizing, condition, and return policies.
  • Filters must be simple: Complex options frustrated 3/5 participants.
  • Emotion enhances retention: Parents felt motivated by sustainability and helping other families.

Storyboard

I created storyboards around Clarke, a caregiver persona, to capture both quick and emotional user journeys. One illustrated Clarke breezing through sign-up, browsing, and checkout; the other highlighted frustration on existing platforms and relief when using Grow Up Green.

Design Process

I began with quick sketches in Pic Collage to visualize layouts, then created low-fidelity wireframes and prototypes for early testing. Iterating into high-fidelity, I ensured responsive design for both mobile and desktop.

Key Design Priorities

  • Effortless navigation with minimal steps and grid-based browsing.
  • Smart recommendations inspired by TikTok‚Äôs logic.
  • User profiles tracking environmental impact.
  • Distinct design languages: bold and fast for mobile, calm and structured for web.

Visual and Accessibility Choices

I used a forest and spring green palette to align with eco-conscious branding. Accessibility was integrated into every decision: icons were labeled, contrast ratios were checked, and layouts were designed with clarity for all users.

Usability Testing and Iteration

What I Observed

  1. Navigation was unclear‚Äîusers often skipped the homepage.
  2. Visual hierarchy needed refinement‚Äîspacing and grouping caused confusion.
  3. Overall usability was high, but delight was missing‚Äîinteractions felt flat.
  4. Checkout performed well‚Äî100% of participants completed it without help.

Iterations Based on Feedback

  • Clearer navigation labels and repositioned search bar.
  • Refined spacing for filters and product cards.
  • Simplified dense screens on mobile for faster scanning.
  • Retested all prototype flows to confirm fixes.

Final Design and Features

  • User profile pages with eco-impact tracking.
  • Simplified item listing flow for busy parents.
  • Responsive layouts optimized for mobile and desktop.
  • Smart filtering, personalized recommendations, and wishlists.
  • Free item section (covering shipping only).
  • Playful, positive design tone reinforcing sustainability.

Unlike most resale platforms, Grow Up Green extends beyond clothing, covering toys and gear while tying purchases to visible environmental impact. This holistic approach fills a meaningful market gap.

Key Takeaways

This project showed me the value of co-designing with users. Observing real interactions revealed insights I wouldn‚Äôt have uncovered otherwise, and testers frequently asked when the app would be available‚Äîvalidating its appeal. I grew significantly in prototyping, iteration, and user story development, and I learned how simplicity and trust can drive engagement in family-focused design.
