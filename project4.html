Overview

Grow Up Green is a responsive, sustainability-driven resale platform designed to make secondhand kids‚Äô shopping intuitive, trustworthy, and emotionally rewarding. The platform blends swipe-style browsing, eco-impact feedback, and accessibility-first interactions to give families a faster, more confidence-building alternative to existing resale apps.

My Role

I owned the full end-to-end design process, including research planning, interviews, competitive analysis, persona creation, low- and high-fidelity prototyping, usability testing, iteration, and branding. I developed the visual identity, color palette, typography system, and responsive component library for both mobile and desktop.

Problem Space

As a first-time aunt watching my sisters navigate constant growth spurts and tight budgets, I saw the real financial pressure of buying new children‚Äôs clothing. My experience reselling on Depop revealed a major gap: no platform truly supports the needs of parents.

Parents expressed frustration with:

  • Time-consuming browsing
  • Unclear clothing conditions
  • Low trust in sellers
  • Overwhelming layouts
  • Lack of emotional incentive to buy used
How might we create a secondhand shopping experience for kids that feels fast, trustworthy, and emotionally rewarding?

Competitive Audit

I evaluated ThredUp, Mercari, Poshmark, and Facebook Marketplace to understand gaps in clarity, accessibility, and trust.

Key Findings

  • Clean layouts exist, but readability and icon clarity are inconsistent.
  • Mobile browsing is strong on some platforms but clutter overwhelms new users.
  • No platform is built specifically for kids‚Äô resale.
  • Product descriptions and seller trust vary dramatically.
  • None incorporate eco-impact or reward systems.

User Research

I conducted five in-person interviews with parents, caregivers, and grandparents. Participants completed shopping tasks including filtering, evaluating product details, and checking out while comparing flows to familiar platforms.

Key Insights

  • Trust drives decisions: Participants wanted seller clarity and transparent item conditions.
  • Efficiency is essential: Every participant said they would leave if they couldn‚Äôt find items quickly.
  • Clean, detailed product cards build confidence.
  • Sustainability offers emotional motivation.

Storyboard

I created frustration and delight storyboards centered around Clarke, a caregiver persona navigating common challenges in kids‚Äô resale. The frustration path highlighted time loss, clutter, unclear conditions, and distrust. The delight path showed a streamlined journey‚Äîclearer details, trustworthy sellers, and eco-impact rewards.

Design Process

I began with rough composition sketches to conceptualize layout logic and flows. Then I developed low-fidelity wireframes exploring homepage hierarchy, product card structure, browsing flows, and listing flows.

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

High-fidelity designs incorporated branded colors, typography, iconography, and responsive spacing. Mobile emphasized bold, swipe-driven interactions; desktop emphasized calm grid-based browsing.

Visual & Accessibility Choices

I used a forest-green and spring-green palette to reinforce sustainability. Accessibility was considered throughout: labeled icons, verified contrast ratios, and simplified filters ensured clarity and reduced cognitive load.

Usability Testing

I conducted a moderated study with five participants testing browsing, filtering, product detail clarity, and checkout.

Top Observations

  • 2/5 struggled with navigation clarity.
  • 1/5 wanted better structural organization.
  • 4/5 said the app felt intuitive.
  • 5/5 completed checkout easily.

What This Meant

  • Navigation required clearer labels and hierarchy.
  • Layout needed more spacing and grouping.
  • Core flow was intuitive but missing delight.
  • Checkout was strong and only required minor polishing.

Iterations Based on Testing

  • Replaced vague icons with labeled ones.
  • Repositioned search bar for visibility.
  • Organized filters to reduce overwhelm.
  • Added eco-impact micro-interactions and subtle animations.

Final Design & Features

  • Eco-impact profile tracking CO‚ÇÇ savings.
  • Smart swipe-style recommendations.
  • One-minute listing experience with guided steps.
  • Responsive layouts for mobile and web.
  • Wishlists and trusted seller bookmarking.
  • Free-items + shipping-only section.

Grow Up Green fills a meaningful gap: clarity, trust, speed, emotional motivation, and accessibility-first design for all families.
