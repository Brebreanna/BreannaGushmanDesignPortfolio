Quizzlr:
Multiplayer Study App

A gamified flashcard platform that turns studying into real-time quiz battles — designed to make learning collaborative, competitive, and worth coming back to.

Role Solo UX/UI Designer
Timeline 24 hours
Team 3 members
Tools Figma, Notion

01

The Problem

⏱ 24-hour hackathon constraint

Students are expected to master large volumes of content in short periods. Traditional study tools feel repetitive and isolating — making it harder to stay engaged or actually retain information.

Design goal

Redesign studying as something collaborative, motivating, and fun — a multiplayer experience where students challenge friends, track growth, and enjoy learning together rather than grinding alone.

02

Research

With 24 hours on the clock, I ran a fast competitive audit of Quizlet, Kahoot, Chegg, and IXL — reviewing features, interfaces, and interaction patterns to identify gaps and opportunities.

What competitors do well
  • Clean flashcard creation flows
  • Progress tracking and streaks
  • Multiple question formats
What the market is missing
  • Real-time multiplayer study modes
  • Social features beyond leaderboards
  • Interfaces that don't overwhelm
  • Personalized deck customization

I supplemented the audit with insights from my software engineering professor and live feedback from my two teammates throughout the build — validating design decisions in real time rather than in isolation.

03

Design Process

I started with quick notebook sketches then jumped straight to high-fidelity Figma designs — skipping lo-fi entirely to maximize time. Every screen was built, reviewed, and iterated on during the hackathon.

Key screens built

🏠 Home Dashboard

Quick access to decks, quiz modes, and leaderboard in one glance.

✏️ Flashcard Creator

Multiple choice and diagram modes for visual learners.

📚 My Decks

Organized access to study sets with simple preview and edit workflow.

⚔️ Quiz Battle

Real-time multiplayer mode with live scoring and opponent progress.

🏆 Leaderboard

XP, ranks, and avatars to gamify engagement and reward consistency.

🖱️ Drag & Drop Builder

Interactive study set customization that feels tactile and intuitive.

04

Design Decisions

  • Clarity over features Every screen had one primary action. Under 24-hour pressure, I cut anything that didn't directly improve the core study or battle experience.
  • Gamification that's actually usable XP, leaderboards, and battle mode only work if they're immediately understandable. Every mechanic was kept simple enough to grasp without onboarding.
  • Two complete user flows Solo flashcard study and multiplayer quiz — both fully clickable by demo time. Scope discipline meant neither flow felt unfinished.
  • Real-time engineer collaboration I shared screens continuously and adjusted spacing, labels, and interactions on the fly based on what was feasible to build in the time remaining.
05

Outcome

We delivered a high-fidelity, clickable prototype with two complete user flows in 24 hours. The app ran smoothly during demo presentation and judges praised both the UI clarity and the core concept.

Judge feedback

Judges confirmed that Quizzlr is more than a hackathon concept — it's something worth building further. That validation made every crunch-time decision feel worth it.

06

What I Learned

01 Pressure builds clarity

When you can't do everything, you quickly learn what actually matters. The essentials became obvious fast.

02 Gamification requires simplicity

Every mechanic — XP, battles, leaderboards — worked because it was easy to understand on first contact, not because it was elaborate.

03 Design for the engineers

Shipping live with teammates means your design decisions directly affect what's buildable. Real-time collaboration changed how I think about handoff.

04 Small interactions shape feel

Leaderboard animations and drag-and-drop weren't extras — they were what made Quizzlr feel like a product, not a prototype.

Quizzlr proved that focused collaboration and intentional UX can deliver something genuinely compelling — even when the clock is running and the scope keeps shrinking.
